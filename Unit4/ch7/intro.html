The Shifting Sands of Deep Time

Before we delve into how life changes, we must understand that the stage upon which life acts—the Earth itself—is also constantly changing.

Interactive: Drag the slider below to watch 250 million years of continental drift. Pay close attention to the glowing orange markers. Notice how fossils of the exact same ancient fern (Glossopteris) are found on the widely separated modern coasts of South America, Africa, Antarctica, India, and Australia.

How could a heavy-seeded fern cross a massive ocean? It didn't. The continents moved.
