The Pristine Forest (Pre-1850s)

Welcome to early 19th-century England. The forests are clean, and the trunks of the birch trees are pale and covered in light-colored lichens.

Living in this forest is the Peppered Moth (Biston betularia). These moths come in two main genetic variations (phenotypes):

Typica (Light Variant)

Pale with dark speckles. This is the most common variant at this time.

Carbonaria (Dark Variant)

Solid dark gray or black. A rare genetic mutation.

As a predatory bird, your survival depends on finding and eating these moths. Let's see how well you can hunt in this environment!

The Industrial Revolution (Late 1800s)

Decades have passed. The Industrial Revolution has transformed England. Factories are burning massive amounts of coal to power new machinery.

Unfortunately, there are no environmental regulations. Heavy black smoke pours from the smokestacks, blanketing the surrounding countryside in thick, dark soot.

Environmental Impact:

  • The air quality plummets, causing the light-colored lichens on the trees to die.
  • The tree bark itself becomes stained dark brown and black from the industrial soot.
  • The visual landscape of the forest has completely inverted.

The peppered moths still live in this forest. As a bird, you must return to hunt. Let's see how the environmental change affects your diet.
