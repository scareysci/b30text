Case Briefing

Welcome, Detective. Last night, the pride of our town—the legendary Golden Wheat Sheaf—was stolen right out of the display case at the Eatonia Heritage Park.

We have a primary suspect pool: The Kerroberts. They are a large family from the rival town up the highway. The 4th generation of cousins (Mick, Nina, Oscar, Penny, Quinn, and Riley) were seen in town last night for a family reunion.

We have compiled a 4-generation pedigree of the Kerrobert family. However, the record is incomplete. Many of the physical traits (phenotypes) are missing and marked with a [?]. You will need to use your knowledge of Mendelian genetics (dominant vs. recessive inheritance) to fill in the blanks and deduce the exact traits of the 4th Generation Suspects.

The Genetic Traits Under Investigation:

  • Earlobe Attachment (E/e): Free-hanging earlobes (Dominant - E) vs. Attached earlobes (Recessive - e).
  • Hairline (W/w): Widow's Peak, a V-shape point (Dominant - W) vs. Straight hairline (Recessive - w).
  • Thumb Mobility (H/h): Straight thumb (Dominant - H) vs. Hitchhiker's thumb (Recessive - h).

Your Mission: Analyze the pedigree. Deduce the phenotypes for all 6 Suspects. Then, follow the clues left at local landmarks to progressively rule out suspects based on their inherited genetics. Finally, deduce specific genotypes and file your official RCMP Police Report.

The Investigation Field Notes

Clue #1: The Eston Riverside Discovery

You receive a call from the Eston detachment. Near the Eston Riverside Regional Park, exactly where the Eston Ramblers held their team wind-up, a torn balaclava was found caught on a Saskatoon berry bush branch. RCMP forensics pulled a single hair follicle from the mask. The root structure and distinct angled growth pattern definitively prove the suspect has a Widow's Peak (W).

Clue #2: The Leader Landmark Sighting

A tourist taking photos at the Giant Western Meadowlark sculpture in Leader reports seeing someone matching the thief's build running to a truck. The tourist snapped a photo just as the suspect gave a sarcastic "thumbs up" gesture. The photograph clearly shows the thumb bent backwards at an extreme 90-degree angle—a classic Hitchhiker's thumb (h).

Clue #3: Foot traffic at the Kindersley Mall

Checking regional cameras, you spot our suspect getting out of their truck at the Kindersley Mall. For a brief second before pulling up their hood, a side-profile reflection is caught in the glass doors of the mall entrance. The image is enhanced: the suspect's earlobes blend directly into the side of their head without any free-hanging loop. They have Attached Earlobes (e).
