Lab Reference Guide

To determine a blood type, look for agglutination (clumping) when reacting with different serums.

  • Anti-A Serum: Clumps if A antigens are present.
  • Anti-B Serum: Clumps if B antigens are present.
  • Anti-Rh Serum: Clumps if Rh+ (positive).

Genetics Quick Ref

  • A & B alleles are dominant over O.
  • A & B are codominant (creating Type AB).
  • Rh+ is dominant over Rh-.
