Understanding Negative Feedback Loops

What is Homeostasis?

Homeostasis is the ability of a system (like your body) to maintain a stable, relatively constant internal environment, despite changes in the outside world.

This stability is achieved using a Negative Feedback Loop. When a change occurs, the system responds in the opposite direction to bring things back to normal.

🌡️

1. Stimulus

A change in the environment (e.g., it gets cold).

📡

2. Sensor

Detects the change and sends a signal.

🧠

3. Control Center

Receives the signal and decides what to do.

⚙️

4. Effector

Takes action to reverse the stimulus.
